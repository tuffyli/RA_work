	{Resisiting the heat: temperature, achievement, and the mediating role of exam stakes}
	{NA}
	{}
	{Temperature effect in high stakes exams}
	
	\item "\textit{The theoretical mediating role of exam stakes is expected to be negative: the higher the exam stakes, the lowe the effects of temperature (Park, 2020).}"
	\item  Findings that one-standard-deviation increase in temperature ($\approx 4$ degree celsius) reduces exam scores by 0.026 sd.
	\item Extreme conditions (above 32 degress) reduces performance by 0.05 sd.
	\item Effect concentrated in poorer municipalities (worst school infrastructure, higher poverty, and lower human development).
	\item As the stakes increased the temperature effect decreased. (-38\% per sd of new university adopting ENEM).
	\item As the stakes increase, students adopt more effectively coping strategies to manage stress and disconfort associated with the heat.
	\\
	
	\textbf{Theoretical Framework}
	\item Decompose utility at the exam time, where a exists a positive relationship between exam score and future wages.
	\item Effort increases scores, effort effects diminishes, and effort is less effective in improving the test scores when temperature is higher.
	\item Trade-off between the benefit and the cost of increasing effort.\\
	\\
	\textbf{Temperature decreases performance}
	\item \textit{"... students may exert different levels of effort at a mock exam and a college entrance exam given that the latter is strongly related to the future income."}.
	\item Temperature effects on the effort level. More effort is made to compensate the temperature effects when the exam is more important.
